Junkyard Joystick

Built primarily out of boxes, bottle caps, and other materials I scavenged from my family's recycling bin over winter break, this joystick is build completely from used materials and basic electronic components.

How it works

Like most digital joysticks, the Junkyard Joystick utilizes four buttons hidden underneath the casing to determine its position. As the stick is pivoted, the bottom pushes buttons positioned in a cross pattern around it. If the left button is pushed, the joystick has been pushed to the right. Similarly the right button's activation signifies movement to the left, up signifies down, and down, up. These push buttons are monitored by an Arduino microcontroller which translates them into mouse movements and delivers commands to a connected computer over a usb connection.

Material Choice and Usage

The materials used to create this joystick were fittingly, from the junkyard. This lead to some interesting innovation, my favorite of which was the use of the milk jug cap. Punching the dowel though the bottom of the cap and securing it with hot glue provided the two necessary degrees of rotational freedom, while restricting translation and axial rotation. I also enjoyed using the soda bottle lid to provide extra width to the bottom of the dowel and vertically fastened perfboard to position the buttons where they would be tapped by that soda lid.
